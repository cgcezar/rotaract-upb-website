About the
Organization

We are a community of young leaders at the University of the Philippines Baguio, united by one idea: that ordinary students can do extraordinary good when they organize, lead, and serve together.

Rotaract — short for “Rotary in Action” — is a global movement of people aged 18 to 30 who develop leadership and professional skills while creating lasting change in their communities. In 2019, Rotaract was elevated from a program to a full membership type of Rotary International, making Rotaractors true partners in service within the Rotary family.

Our club was chartered to bring this spirit to UP Baguio. From campus initiatives to district-wide campaigns, we work hand in hand with our sponsoring Rotary club to serve where we are needed most.

End Polio
Now

The eradication of polio is one of Rotary's longest-standing and most significant commitments — and it began in the Philippines.

In 1979, Rotary's very first polio project vaccinated children right here in the Philippines. Inspired by that success, Rotary became a founding partner of the Global Polio Eradication Initiative in 1988. Since then, polio cases worldwide have fallen by 99.9%, and more than 3 billion children across 122 countries have been protected from the disease.

Today, wild polio remains endemic in only two countries. But the work is not finished — and as a Rotaract club, the fight to End Polio is ours to carry forward through advocacy, awareness, and fundraising.

Our Rotary
Sponsor

Sponsoring Rotary Club

Rotary Club of Baguio North

Every Rotaract club is guided by a sponsoring Rotary club — mentors and partners in service. Ours is the Rotary Club of Baguio North, part of Rotary International District 3790, the district of clubs serving Northern Luzon.

Rotary Club of Baguio North is known for community-driven initiatives such as its “Limitless Project: Thriving Together,” which supports families of children with special needs. As our sponsor, they offer guidance, resources, and a tradition of service for us to build on.
